## VI. CAREER PIVOT: FROM TECHNOLOGY TO CAREGIVING

The second DUI was the catalyst that transformed my career trajectory. In the weeks and months following January 17, 2024, as I engaged in intensive rehabilitation at Harmony Grove Recovery and deep self-reflection through counseling, AA, and the 18-month DUI program, I arrived at a conviction that has only strengthened with time: **I want to dedicate the remainder of my professional life to the service and protection of others — specifically, California's elderly population.**

This was not a sudden, impulsive decision. It was the product of months of clinical work, honest self-assessment, and the recognition that my experiences — both my professional capabilities and my personal struggles — position me to serve in a way that is both competent and deeply empathetic.

### Why the Elderly?

Working at Uni Care — a home health, hospice, and home care organization — I have spent over five years in an environment centered on the care of vulnerable populations, including the elderly. While my role has been in IT, I have been immersed in the mission, language, and culture of caregiving every day. I have witnessed:

- The dedication of nurses and aides who provide dignified, compassionate care to patients in their final stages of life
- The profound impact that a well-run care facility has on the quality of life, dignity, and safety of elderly residents
- The critical importance of regulatory compliance, staff training, and operational excellence in protecting vulnerable populations
- The gaps that exist when facilities lack administrators who combine operational competence with genuine compassion

I have seen what excellent care looks like, and I have seen the consequences when systems fail. I want to be part of the solution — not as a technologist supporting care from behind a screen, but as a licensed administrator directly responsible for the safety, dignity, and well-being of elderly residents.

### The "Bridge" — Why My Background Is a Strength

I recognize that a career pivot from IT management to RCFE administration is unconventional, and I want to address directly why I believe my background is not a limitation but a distinctive asset:

**From IT — The Structure:**
- **Regulatory Compliance:** I have managed HIPAA-compliant systems for a healthcare organization for over five years. I understand the rigor, documentation, and audit-readiness that regulatory environments demand. Title 22 compliance, CCLD inspections, and CPMB oversight require the same systematic discipline.
- **Systems Thinking:** Managing IT infrastructure for 300+ employees has taught me to think in systems — to anticipate failures, build redundancies, create protocols, and ensure continuity of operations. These are the same skills required to manage staffing, medication administration, emergency procedures, and resident care plans in an RCFE.
- **Attention to Detail:** In IT, a single misconfiguration can compromise patient data or disable clinical operations. I bring that same zero-tolerance approach to detail into facility management.
- **Staff Coordination:** I have coordinated with directors, managers, nurses, therapists, social workers, and frontline aides. I understand multidisciplinary team dynamics and how to support each role effectively.

**From CNA/Nursing Training — The Heart:**
- **Hands-On Clinical Skills:** My CNA training — which I am completing within approximately two weeks of this submission — has given me direct, hands-on experience with activities of daily living (ADLs), patient transfers, vital signs, infection control, nutrition, and the intimate realities of caring for individuals who depend on others for their basic needs.
- **Empathy Through Experience:** My rehabilitation journey — the vulnerability of asking for help, the humility of starting over, the gratitude of being given a second chance — has given me a depth of empathy that cannot be learned from a textbook. I understand what it feels like to be in a position of vulnerability, and I carry that understanding into every interaction with those in my care.
- **Medical Knowledge:** My CNA training and upcoming RN program at Palomar College will provide the clinical foundation necessary to understand and oversee the medical needs of elderly residents.

**The Synthesis:**
RCFE administration demands both operational excellence and genuine compassion. Most administrators come from one background or the other — they are either operationally strong but clinically distant, or clinically empathetic but administratively overwhelmed. My trajectory provides both. I bring the systems, the compliance expertise, and the organizational discipline from IT — and I bring the clinical training, the empathy born of personal struggle, and the deep motivation of someone who has chosen this path with intentionality and purpose.
